01 — The Opportunity

Growing food and returns
in the same soil

Mirath Farms is a UAE regenerative agricultural cooperative offering investors direct ownership of a productive food business — with a guaranteed exit return, equity upside, and the infrastructure for long-term profit sharing as the cooperative grows.

The cooperative begins with a controlled indoor lab producing exotic mushrooms and specialty chillies for UAE hospitality. This is not a concept — production capacity is documented, channel pricing is verified, and a hotel offtake partnership is already in place. From this foundation we expand across three phases into a full diversified farm: poultry, orchards, composting, and a creative hub.

Total cooperative capitalisation target is AED 10,000,000. Phase 1 raises AED 500,000 to establish and operate the lab, prove the model, and generate the first investor returns.

Mirath Farms enters at the optimal moment: early enough for meaningful equity upside, with verified infrastructure that eliminates concept-stage risk.

What 4.2× coverage means in practice

At the absolute worst-case scenario — 40% of documented capacity, minimal high-margin product mix — the cooperative generates AED 1,262,790. The total investor return obligation on a full AED 500,000 raise is AED 150,000. Net profit at the floor still covers that obligation more than four times over. The farm does not need to perform well to return investor capital. It needs to operate.

Revenue channels

Phase 1 revenue comes from two documented streams with verified pricing:

  • Exotic mushroom cultivation — oyster, shiitake, and lion's mane varieties sold to UAE hospitality. Hotel offtake partnership established. Premium fresh and dried product mix.
  • Specialty chilli production — fresh wholesale and high-margin sauce/condiment product line. Sauce mix percentage is the primary upside lever — moving from 20% to 70% sauce mix is the single largest driver of revenue above floor.

05 — Governance & Rights

Ownership rights that
are actually enforced

Cooperative ownership instruments in the UAE are traditionally illiquid and opaque. Mirath Farms is building the infrastructure to change that — starting with our own cooperative.

Every governance right, profit share entitlement, and ownership transfer in Mirath Farms is recorded and enforced automatically. This is not administered by a spreadsheet or a solicitor's letter — it is encoded into the cooperative's operating infrastructure from day one.

Member rights

  • Annual profit share distributed automatically to all holders
  • Voting rights on major asset decisions proportional to stake
  • Consent rights on major asset sales — no founder unilateral disposal
  • Transparent ownership record — auditable at any time

Founder obligations

  • Founder equity is time-locked — cannot be sold during the lock-up period
  • Lock-up enforced automatically — not a contractual undertaking
  • All distributions recorded and publicly auditable
  • Supermajority member consent required for material decisions

Secondary market

Equity and profit-share positions can be traded between verified cooperative members without requiring a centralised intermediary. All trades settle between identity-verified parties — the cooperative maintains compliance on every transfer, not just at the point of entry.

06 — Risk Disclosure

Honest risks,
honestly stated

This document does not constitute a financial prospectus or regulated investment advice. All investment carries risk. The following is a non-exhaustive summary of material risks investors should consider before participating.

Agricultural
Phase 1 uses indoor controlled lab production, reducing weather and seasonal exposure. Crop disease or equipment failure could reduce output below documented floor projections. Diversification across two product lines reduces single-crop concentration risk.
Execution
Mirath Farms is a pre-revenue business. All projections are based on documented production capacity and verified channel pricing, not historical trading results. Management execution risk is material at this stage.
Market
Hospitality sector demand for premium produce could soften. The cooperative's product mix flexibility (wholesale vs. sauce) provides a natural hedge — lower-margin wholesale is the floor, higher-margin sauce production is the upside lever.
Regulatory
UAE regulatory treatment of digital cooperative ownership instruments is evolving. We will engage qualified UAE legal counsel prior to any public offering. Phase 1 operates under a standard SAFE instrument with documented terms.
Liquidity
Cooperative equity is not publicly traded. Secondary market liquidity depends on the size of the verified member pool. Option A investors have a clean exit at month 12 regardless of secondary market conditions.
Shariah
The community profit-share instrument is designed with Shariah alignment as a long-term goal. Formal certification from a recognised scholar will be pursued post-Phase 1. No Shariah compliance certification is claimed at this stage.
